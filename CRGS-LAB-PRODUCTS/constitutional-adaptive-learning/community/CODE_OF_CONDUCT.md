# Code of Conduct — Constitutional Adaptive Learning Systems Protocol

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** June 23, 2026  
**Last Updated:** June 23, 2026

---

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the Constitutional Adaptive Learning Systems Protocol (CALSP) community pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Age
- Body size
- Visible or invisible disability
- Ethnicity
- Sex characteristics
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Education
- Socio-economic status
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Caste
- Color
- Religion
- Sexual identity and orientation
- **Neurodivergence** (autism, ADHD, dyslexia, etc.)
- Communication style
- Cognitive processing differences

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community that **centers neurodivergent voices and experiences**.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

✅ **Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people**
- Assume good intent
- Be patient with different communication styles
- Offer help without condescension

✅ **Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences**
- Engage in constructive disagreement
- Acknowledge that there are multiple valid perspectives
- Avoid dismissing lived experiences

✅ **Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback**
- Focus on the work, not the person
- Be specific and actionable
- Accept feedback without defensiveness

✅ **Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes**
- Acknowledge harm caused
- Learn from mistakes
- Make amends when possible

✅ **Focusing on what is best for the overall community**
- Prioritize collective benefit over individual gain
- Support community governance decisions
- Contribute to a sustainable project

✅ **Using neurodivergent-affirming language**
- Avoid deficit-based language (e.g., "suffers from autism")
- Use identity-first language when preferred (e.g., "autistic person")
- Respect individual language preferences
- Challenge ableist assumptions

✅ **Accommodating diverse communication needs**
- Provide content in multiple formats (text, audio, visual)
- Allow extra processing time
- Use direct, literal language
- Offer tone indicators when helpful

✅ **Respecting sensory and cognitive boundaries**
- Avoid flashing images or sudden loud sounds
- Provide content warnings for potentially overwhelming material
- Respect "Do Not Disturb" status
- Allow breaks and flexible participation

---

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

❌ **Harassment and discrimination**
- Offensive comments related to any protected characteristic
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Sustained disruption of discussions or events

❌ **Ableism and neurodivergent discrimination**
- Dismissing or mocking neurodivergent experiences
- Using "autistic" or "ADHD" as insults
- Demanding that neurodivergent people "act normal"
- Refusing reasonable accommodations
- Tone policing or communication style criticism
- Assuming incompetence based on disability

❌ **Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments**
- Personal attacks
- Political or religious attacks unrelated to the project
- Public or private harassment

❌ **Publishing others' private information**
- Sharing physical or email addresses without permission
- Doxxing or threats of doxxing
- Sharing private communications publicly

❌ **Inappropriate use of nudity, sexual imagery, or language**
- In any project space, including code comments and commit messages

❌ **Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate**
- In a professional or community setting
- As determined by community moderators

## Neurodivergent-First Principles

This Code of Conduct is grounded in neurodivergent-first values:

### 1. Communication Diversity is Valued
- Direct communication is not rude
- Silence is not agreement or disagreement
- Different processing speeds are respected
- Multiple communication modes are supported (text, voice, visual)

### 2. Sensory Needs are Accommodated
- No one is required to participate in video calls
- Flashing images and sudden sounds are prohibited
- Quiet spaces are provided at in-person events
- Stimming and fidgeting are welcomed

### 3. Executive Function Support is Provided
- Deadlines are flexible when possible
- Tasks are broken into manageable steps
- Reminders are offered without judgment
- "I forgot" is a valid reason

### 4. Masking is Not Required
- Neurodivergent people can be themselves
- No pressure to make eye contact or small talk
- Scripting and rehearsed responses are fine
- Authenticity is valued over "professionalism"

### 5. Lived Experience is Expertise
- Neurodivergent voices are prioritized in decisions affecting neurodivergent people
- "Nothing about us without us" is enforced
- Disagreement with neurodivergent perspectives requires strong justification

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

### Enforcement Team

- **Community Manager:** Primary point of contact
- **Neurodivergent Advisory Board (NAB):** Reviews all enforcement decisions affecting neurodivergent community members
- **Community Governance Board (CGB):** Appeals and complex cases
- **Independent Mediator:** Available for conflicts involving leadership

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Discord server
- Email lists
- Social media (Twitter, Mastodon, etc.)
- In-person events (conferences, hackathons, meetings)
- One-on-one communications when representing the project

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples include using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an event.

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it as soon as possible:

**Primary Contact:**
- **Email:** conduct@calsp.org
- **Discord:** DM @CommunityManager
- **Anonymous Form:** [https://forms.gle/calsp-conduct](https://forms.gle/calsp-conduct)

**Alternative Contacts (if primary contact is involved):**
- **Neurodivergent Co-Lead:** ncl@calsp.org
- **Principal Investigator:** pi@calsp.org
- **Independent Mediator:** mediator@calsp.org

### What to Include in Your Report

Please include as much of the following information as possible:

- Your contact information (optional for anonymous reports)
- Names (usernames, real names) of individuals involved
- Description of the incident, including:
  - What happened
  - When and where it happened
  - Context and background
  - Any witnesses
- Links to relevant messages, issues, or commits
- Any other relevant information

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion. We will not share your identity without your explicit permission, except when required by law or to protect others from harm.

---

## Enforcement Process

### 1. Acknowledgment (Within 24 Hours)

The enforcement team will acknowledge receipt of your report within 24 hours.

### 2. Investigation (Within 7 Days)

The enforcement team will:
- Review the report and gather additional information
- Interview involved parties (if appropriate)
- Consult with NAB if the incident involves neurodivergent community members
- Determine if a Code of Conduct violation occurred

### 3. Decision (Within 14 Days)

Based on the investigation, the enforcement team will determine appropriate consequences (see Enforcement Guidelines below).

### 4. Communication

All parties will be informed of the decision and rationale. The reporter will be notified of the outcome (within confidentiality constraints).

### 5. Appeal (Within 30 Days)

Any party may appeal the decision to the Community Governance Board (CGB) within 30 days. Appeals must include new information or demonstrate procedural errors.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines in determining consequences:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

**Example:** Using ableist language unintentionally; corrected when pointed out.

---

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time (typically 30 days). This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

**Example:** Repeatedly dismissing neurodivergent perspectives after being asked to stop.

---

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time (typically 90 days). No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

**Example:** Harassment, doxxing, or sustained ableist behavior.

---

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

**Example:** Severe harassment, threats of violence, or repeated violations after temporary ban.

---

## Restorative Justice Approach

When appropriate and agreed upon by all parties, we may use restorative justice practices instead of or in addition to punitive measures. This may include:

- Facilitated dialogue between parties
- Community service (e.g., improving documentation)
- Educational requirements (e.g., reading about neurodiversity)
- Mentorship or coaching

Restorative justice is **never required** of the person harmed and is only used when all parties consent.

---

## Accountability for Leadership

Community leaders and project maintainers are held to a **higher standard** and may face more severe consequences for Code of Conduct violations, including:

- Immediate removal from leadership positions
- Public acknowledgment of violations
- Permanent ban from leadership roles

The Neurodivergent Advisory Board (NAB) has the authority to remove any community leader who violates neurodivergent-first principles.
