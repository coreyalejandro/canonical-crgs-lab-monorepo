# Minimal Ethics Checklist for Bootstrapped Pilot

**Version:** 4.3  
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-23  
**Purpose:** Pre-submission planning tool for IRB exemption request

---

## ⚠️ CRITICAL NOTICE

**THIS CHECKLIST IS A PLANNING TOOL ONLY. IT DOES NOT REPLACE INSTITUTIONAL IRB REVIEW.**

You MUST submit your protocol to your institution's IRB and receive formal approval or exemption determination before collecting any human subjects data. This checklist helps you prepare for that submission.

**No data collection may begin until you have documented IRB approval.**

---

## How to Use This Checklist

1. ✅ Check each item as you complete it
2. 📝 Add notes in the "Evidence/Notes" column
3. 🔍 Review with your faculty advisor or IRB coordinator
4. 📤 Submit complete protocol to IRB
5. ⏳ Wait for IRB determination before recruiting participants

## Common IRB Questions — Be Prepared to Answer

1. **Why is this research important?**
   - Answer: Advances understanding of constitutional AI for cognitive diversity; informs neurodivergent-first design.

2. **Why do you need human participants?**
   - Answer: Cannot assess cognitive load, trust, and usability without human interaction.

3. **Why N=10?**
   - Answer: Pilot study for feasibility testing and instrument validation before larger megaproject.

4. **How will you protect neurodivergent participants?**
   - Answer: Accessible design, sensory accommodations, control over participation, neurodivergent co-design.

5. **What if a participant becomes distressed?**
   - Answer: Immediate pause option, contact info for support, report to IRB within 24 hours, stopping rules in place.

6. **How will you ensure data security?**
   - Answer: Encrypted storage, access-restricted, no unnecessary PII, separate storage of identifiers.

7. **What will you do with the data after the study?**
   - Answer: De-identify and share publicly on Zenodo; participants can opt out of data sharing.

8. **How is this different from a class project?**
   - Answer: Systematic research with preregistered plan, publication intent, contribution to scientific knowledge.

## Red Flags — Do NOT Proceed If:

🚫 You plan to collect data before IRB approval  
🚫 You cannot explain how the research benefits participants or society  
🚫 You are using deception without justification  
🚫 You are collecting sensitive information (health, financial, criminal) without strong protections  
🚫 You cannot guarantee data security  
🚫 You are coercing participants (excessive compensation, pressure from authority figure)  
🚫 You have not completed human subjects research training  
🚫 You do not have a faculty advisor (if you are a student)  

**If any of these apply, STOP and consult with your IRB office before proceeding.**
