## Intellectual Merit

### Research Innovation

This project introduces three major theoretical and methodological innovations:

1. **Constitutional Adaptive Learning Theory (CALT)** — A unified framework integrating constitutional AI safety, neurodiversity-affirming design, and trust calibration. CALT advances AI safety research by demonstrating how constitutional constraints can coexist with dynamic adaptation to cognitive diversity.

2. **Constitutional Adaptive Mixed-Methods (CAMM) Protocol** — A novel research methodology combining continuous adversarial chaos injection, 24/7 human audit (CHAE), and multi-group structural equation modeling. CAMM advances mixed-methods research by operationalizing real-time ethical oversight as a core methodological component.

3. **Bootstrapped-to-Megaproject Pipeline** — A de-risked scaling approach that validates constructs and instruments at minimal cost (<$1,000) before committing to large-scale investment ($7.2M). This innovation advances research methodology by demonstrating how open-source prototyping can reduce risk in large-scale human subjects research.

### Scientific Contributions

The project makes significant contributions across multiple disciplines:

- **AI Safety:** First large-scale validation of constitutional AI with continuous adversarial testing and human oversight (CHAE) in an educational context.

- **Cognitive Science:** Novel measurement of intra-individual cognitive load variance as a function of adaptive interface design, advancing understanding of cognitive diversity.

- **Human-Computer Interaction:** Cross-cultural validation of trust calibration instruments (DTCI) and usability metrics (SUS) across four global hubs with diverse neurodivergent populations.

- **Learning Sciences:** 365-day retention testing of constitutionally-adapted instruction, advancing understanding of long-term learning outcomes for neurodivergent learners.

- **AI Engineering:** Production-grade architecture (CCMA engine) with p99 latency <150ms under concurrent load, advancing real-time adaptive systems.

### Falsifiable Hypotheses

CALT is expressed through eight preregistered, falsifiable hypotheses (H1-H8) that will be tested using multi-group structural equation modeling with power >.95:

- **H1:** Adaptive interfaces reduce cognitive load variance for neurodivergent learners
- **H2:** Trust calibration improves with NAP-driven adaptation
- **H3:** Continuous CHAE oversight maintains Constitutional Compliance Index (CCI) >0.95
- **H4:** Instruments show cross-cultural measurement invariance
- **H5:** Neurodivergent co-leadership improves retention and community influence
- **H6:** Bootstrapped pilot effect sizes generalize to megaproject
- **H7:** Community contributions correlate with CCI stability
- **H8:** CHAE detects unique safety events missed by automation

### Methodological Rigor

- **Sample:** N=1,000 participants across four culturally distinct global hubs (North America, Western Europe, South Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa)
- **Design:** Concurrent embedded mixed-methods with within-subjects experimental component
- **Analysis:** Multi-group SEM with Bayesian sensitivity analysis, preregistered on OSF
- **Validation:** Cross-cultural measurement invariance testing (configural, metric, scalar)
- **Reproducibility:** Complete open-source reproducibility package with one-click deployment

## Broader Impacts

### Broadening Participation of Underrepresented Groups

This project embeds **neurodivergent-first governance** as a structural model for inclusive AI research:

1. **Neurodivergent Co-Leadership:** A full-time Neurodivergent Co-Lead (NCL) holds equal authority to the PI, ensuring neurodivergent perspectives shape all research decisions.

2. **Neurodivergent Advisory Board (NAB):** 8-10 neurodivergent advisors with **binding veto power** over research design, instruments, and dissemination. This is not advisory—it is co-governance.

3. **Continuous Human Audit Engine (CHAE):** 24/7 rotating panel with neurodivergent representation, providing real-time ethical oversight and demonstrating how human-in-the-loop safety can be operationalized at scale.

4. **Recruitment Quotas:** 50% of participants self-identify as neurodivergent, with explicit representation across at least three distinct cognitive profiles (autism, ADHD, dyslexia, etc.).

This governance structure directly addresses NSF's goal of broadening participation of persons with disabilities in STEM by treating neurodivergent individuals as **co-creators and decision-makers**, not just research subjects.

### Enhancing Public Scientific Literacy

1. **Open-Source Starter Kit:** Complete bootstrapped prototype (<$1,000) enables any researcher, educator, or citizen scientist to replicate the core protocol. This democratizes access to cutting-edge AI research.

2. **Public Dashboards:** Real-time Constitutional Compliance Index (CCI) and trust calibration metrics displayed on public dashboard, making AI safety transparent and understandable to non-experts.

3. **Accessible Documentation:** All materials written in plain language (8th-grade reading level), with visual guides, video tutorials, and sign-language interpretation.

4. **Community Science:** Open contribution pipeline allows community members to propose constitutional rule changes, instrument improvements, and research questions, fostering public engagement with AI ethics.

### Developing a Diverse STEM Workforce

1. **Training Opportunities:** Graduate research assistants (4 FTE) and postdoctoral researchers (2 FTE) receive interdisciplinary training in AI safety, neurodiversity, mixed-methods research, and open-source development.

2. **Neurodivergent Career Pathways:** The Neurodivergent Co-Lead position creates a career pathway for neurodivergent researchers in AI, demonstrating that lived experience is a research qualification.

3. **Open Educational Resources:** All research protocols, instruments, and analysis scripts released as OER, enabling educators worldwide to teach constitutional AI and neurodiversity-affirming design.

4. **Industry Partnerships:** Collaboration with assistive technology companies (e.g., Microsoft Accessibility, Google AI for Social Good) facilitates technology transfer and creates internship opportunities.

### Advancing Societal Well-Being

1. **Ethical AI Design:** Demonstrates how AI systems can be both safe (constitutional) and inclusive (adaptive to cognitive diversity), providing a model for responsible AI development.

2. **Neurodiversity Advocacy:** Challenges deficit-based models of neurodivergence by centering neurodivergent voices in AI design, contributing to broader disability justice movements.

3. **Global Equity:** Four-hub design ensures research benefits are not limited to Western, high-resource contexts. Partnerships with community organizations in Kenya and India advance global AI equity.

## Research Approach

### Methodology: CAMM Protocol

The Constitutional Adaptive Mixed-Methods (CAMM) protocol combines:

1. **Quantitative Component:**
   - Multi-group structural equation modeling (N=1,000)
   - Within-subjects experimental design (adaptive vs. static interfaces)
   - Longitudinal measurement (immediate, 2-week, 365-day retention)
   - Cross-cultural invariance testing

2. **Qualitative Component:**
   - Think-aloud protocols (n=60, 15 per hub)
   - AI-assisted thematic analysis of open-ended feedback
   - Governance meeting minutes and CHAE audit logs
   - Community contribution analysis (GitHub pull requests)

3. **Continuous Safety Monitoring:**
   - Automated adversarial chaos injection (daily)
   - 24/7 CHAE human oversight
   - Real-time Constitutional Compliance Index (CCI)
   - Alignment faking stress tests (weekly)

### Phased Implementation

**Phase 0: Bootstrapped Pilot (Months 1-6, <$1,000)**
- Deploy open-source prototype
- Recruit N=10 participants (IRB exemption)
- Validate instruments (m-NAP, m-DTCI)
- Refine analysis plan
- Publish pilot results on OSF

**Phase 1: Megaproject Preparation (Months 7-12)**
- Secure funding ($7.2M)
- Establish governance (NAB, CGB, CHAE)
- Set up four global hubs
- Translate instruments
- Deploy production infrastructure

**Phase 2: Data Collection (Months 13-24)**
- Recruit N=1,000 participants
- Continuous monitoring and adaptation
- Weekly CHAE reviews
- Monthly governance meetings

**Phase 3: Analysis and Dissemination (Months 25-36)**
- Multi-group SEM analysis
- Qualitative thematic analysis
- Cross-cultural validation
- Publish in top-tier venues (CHI, AAAI, Nature Human Behaviour)
- Release final open-source toolkit
